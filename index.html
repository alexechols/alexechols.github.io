About me

I am a senior at Carnegie Mellon University studying Materials Science and Engineering. My technical interests include advanced manufacturing techniques, polymer science, mechanism design, and optics. Outside of class, I partake in amateur theater, where I specialize in design and construction of theatrical sets.

I have a passion for multidisciplinary projects and love to research advanced technical topics. Some personal projects of mine include the design of laser driver circuitry for an in-progress DIY Selective Laser Melting 3D printer, and a suite of tools for interfacing with the game Hollow Knight. You can read more about these projects below.

Professionally, I have worked in a number of areas, from the design of tooling to automatically cut sheet metal to mechanical testing and characterization of additively manufactured parts. Additionally, I have experience in software development both in industry and in academic research, with a variety of languages including Python, C++, and C#.
