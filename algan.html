Thermal Properties of AlGaN using MD

Computational materials science, particularly molecular dynamics (MD) has been a large interest of mine throughout my undergraduate experience. The summer after my Junior year, I was able to work with the McGaughey group at CMU to perform MD simulations on semiconductor systems with the specific goal of understanding thermal conductivity in Aluminum Gallium Nitrate (AlGaN) alloys. Getting hands on work in academic research has been an extremely valuable experience to me, and this project has allowed me to understand computational techniques and hone my C++ skills in a practical setting.

My work focused on isolating the components of disorder in alloys, specifically differences in local environments with respect to mass, and with respect to interatomic potentials. After learning the basics of MD simulations, I spent the majority of my time directly editing a standard MD code's source (LAMMPS), and using my modified code to perform large scale simulations through the Pittsburgh Supercomputing Center. I implmented several novel interpolation methods for atomic masses and interatomic force constants, and am fortunate enough to be a co-author on a conference paper with the group I was a part of (details to come).
